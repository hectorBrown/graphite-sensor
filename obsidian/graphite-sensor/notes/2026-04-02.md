- Analysed sensor readings, first at 10ms time steps, then 5ms - saw trace of ~50Hz noise on 10ms readings but rate not high enough so stepped up. 50Hz noise very clear on 5ms readings.

![[sensor1-idle-hist.png]]
The standard deviation here is ~18.64, average ADC out is ~2830.32, which creates a "signal to noise ratio" of  151.82 (obviously ignoring that we don't use the full range of ADC output yet -- but encouraging)

![[laptop-groundloop-demo.png]]
This makes it relatively clear that there is a ground loop when the ESP32 is powered over USB while the laptop is plugged in to AC power. Best way to remove this would be to use a buck converter to step down the battery power to 5V for the ESP32 power -- and communicate over bluetooth serial connection, this also avoids any other complications we get from dirty laptop power, and makes the whole module portable.

Further accuracy from moving away from the trans-impedance amplifier to a wheatstone bridge + instrumentation amp setup -- this would require getting a new IC for the inamp as building one would not be precise enough for good measurement, setting the gain right here would let us use the full range of the ADC.
